# Blog Linking Instructions

---

## Freshness & Staleness

1. **6-month rule — internal links.** No internal links to posts older than 6 months unless that post has been reviewed and re-dated within the last 6 months. An old publish date on a linked post reflects on the credibility of the linking post.

2. **6-month rule — external data and reports.** No external links to data, reports, or statistics older than 6 months. If a newer edition of the same report exists, link to that. If no update exists, the data point must be removed or sourced elsewhere.

3. **Regulatory and policy links must be version-confirmed.** No references to government circulars, regulatory guidelines, or scheme documentation without confirming the linked version is current. Regulations change; an outdated circular linked with confidence is a trust liability.

4. **Date-stamp all data points inline.** Every statistic or data point must carry an inline source attribution with the publication period — e.g. *(World Bank, 2024)* or *(CRIF, Q3 2024)*. This makes every claim auditable during reviews and signals currency to both readers and AI engines.

5. **No competition pages.** Under no circumstances should any post link to a competitor's website, product page, comparison page, or any commercially motivated third-party content. This applies to internal mentions as well — do not name and link competitors even in a neutral context.

---

## Approved External Link Sources

External links must come from one of the following tiers. Anything outside these tiers requires explicit sign-off before use.

### Tier 1 — Regulatory & Government Bodies

Link to the official body relevant to the client's sector. The principle: any official government body, statutory regulator, or intergovernmental organisation relevant to the client's industry qualifies as Tier 1.

| Sector | Approved Sources |
|---|---|
| Fintech / Financial Services | RBI, SEBI, IRDAI, MSME Ministry, CRIF, Sahamati |
| Business / Legal / Corporate | MCA, DPIIT, Competition Commission of India, Bar Council publications |
| Healthcare | NMC, CDSCO, Ministry of Health & Family Welfare |
| Cross-sector / General | NITI Aayog, PIB, india.gov.in |
| International | World Bank, IMF, IFC, BIS |

### Tier 2 — Authority Publications

Consulting, legal, and academic publications with established editorial standards.

- **Consulting:** McKinsey & Company, BCG, Deloitte Insights, PwC, EY, KPMG
- **Academic / Business:** Harvard Business Review, MIT Sloan Management Review
- **Legal:** Recognised legal firm publications and law review journals

### Tier 3 — Established Financial & Business Media

- Mint, Economic Times, Business Standard
- Reuters, Bloomberg, Financial Times
- The Ken, Livemint

### Blocked — Do Not Link

- Competitor websites or product pages
- Competitor comparison or alternative pages
- Commercially motivated third-party content (affiliate sites, sponsored content hubs, lead-gen aggregators)
- Personal blogs, LinkedIn articles, or social media posts
- Wikipedia (as a primary source — acceptable only to verify a definition, never for data)

## Anchor Text Rules

6. **Anchor text describes the destination, not the action.** Never use "click here", "read more", "this article", "here", or "this post" as anchor text. The linked phrase must tell the reader — and the search engine — what they will find at the destination.
   - Correct: `the [RBI's credit information guidelines](link) outline the reporting timelines`
   - Incorrect: `read more about this [here](link)`

7. **No duplicate anchor text within a post.** No two links in the same post should share identical anchor text unless they point to the same URL. Duplicate anchors confuse both readers and crawlers.

8. **Avoid over-optimised anchors.** Exact-match keyword anchors on every internal link is a pattern search engines flag. Vary the phrasing while keeping the destination clear.

---

## Link Destination Rules

9. **Internal links must point to the canonical URL.** Never link to a tag page, category page, paginated version, or URL with tracking parameters. One post, one URL.

10. **Do not link to stubs.** No linking to an internal post that is below the minimum word count or marked as a draft. If it is not ready to be read, it is not ready to be linked.

11. **Space out links to the same destination.** No two consecutive paragraphs should both carry a link to the same URL. If the same post is being referenced twice in close proximity, consolidate the reference into one link.

12. **External links must open in a new tab** with `rel="noopener"` on the link element. A reader following an external link must not be taken away from the post entirely.

---

## Link Balance

13. **Internal links must outnumber external links in every post.** This keeps link equity within the cluster and reinforces the site's topical authority.

14. **Maximum 1 external link per 300 words.** External links should feel earned and purposeful, not scattered throughout for the appearance of research.

15. **No affiliate or sponsored links in editorial blog content.** If commercial links exist for any reason, they must carry `rel="sponsored"`. Editorial posts and commercial posts must remain structurally separate.

---

## Audit & Maintenance

16. **Check all links at the time of publish.** Links are written at draft stage and published weeks later. Every link must be re-verified at the moment of publishing — not assumed to still be valid.

17. **Broken link check before every post goes live.** A 404 on an internal link is a crawl signal problem, not just a reader experience issue. No post goes live with a broken link.

18. **Quarterly link audit on all live posts.** Review all posts for: broken links, redirect chains, links pointing to posts that have since been updated with a new URL, and external links where the source has been updated or retracted.

19. **Update before removing redirects.** If a linked post is deleted or merged into another URL, every post linking to the old URL must be updated before the redirect is removed. A redirect chain is a crawl cost; a 404 is worse.
