# Blog Writing Instructions

---

## Writing & Tone

1. **Scenario-led openings only.** Every post opens with a real-world situation the reader recognises — not a definition, not a statistic, not a question directed at the reader.

2. **Third-person for all brand posts and blogs.** No "we", "our", or "you" in the narrative body. Reserve second-person only for CTAs if absolutely necessary.

3. **Technically precise but never jargon-heavy.** The reader is a credit professional, not an academic. Define technical terms on first use; never assume familiarity with regulatory shorthand.

4. **No superlatives without proof.** "The only" and "the best" require a cited source or a verifiable product claim. If it cannot be substantiated, rewrite it.

---

## SEO Fundamentals

5. **Brand name in paragraph 1 — non-negotiable.** The brand name must appear in the first paragraph of every blog post, not buried in CTAs or the author bio. Root cause confirmed: posts where the brand appeared only in CTAs were crawled as sources but the brand was not cited in AI responses.

6. **Target keyword placement.** The target keyword must appear in:
   - H1
   - First 100 words of body copy
   - At least one H2
   - Meta description

7. **Minimum 2 internal links per post** to existing posts within the same content cluster.

8. **Cornerstone link-back rule.** Every supporting piece and cluster post must link back to the relevant Pillar cornerstone once it exists.

9. **Minimum 1 external authoritative link per post.** Approved sources depend on the client's jurisdiction — see Step 3 of the overnight writer prompt for the full geography-indexed source list. Global sources (World Bank, IMF, WHO, McKinsey, Gartner, etc.) are valid for any client. Competitor sites are not acceptable external links under any circumstances.

9a. **External links must carry data, not just presence.** If an external link is specified in the content queue, fetch the page and extract a minimum of 2 specific data points — figures, percentages, dated findings, or named statistics — before writing begins. These must be woven into the article body with inline attribution. The link is embedded at the sentence where its data appears. A link that sits in the article without any of its content being used does not satisfy this rule.

10. **Meta description:** 150–160 characters. Must include the target keyword and a clear value proposition or implied action. Do not auto-generate — write it deliberately.

11. **Slug format:** Short, keyword-rich, no stop words.
    - Correct: `/bureau-score-vs-cibil`
    - Incorrect: `/what-is-the-difference-between-bureau-score-and-cibil-score`

12. **Title tag ≠ H1.** Both must contain the target keyword, but they should be worded differently — the title tag is optimised for search result CTR; the H1 is optimised for on-page clarity.

13. **Alt text on all images.** Descriptive and keyword-relevant where natural. Never keyword-stuffed. An empty alt attribute is not acceptable.

14. **Schema markup:**
    - Article schema on all blog posts
    - FAQ schema wherever a Q&A block exists
    - HowTo schema on all process-oriented posts

---

## Word Count Minimums

| Post Type | Minimum Word Count |
|---|---|
| Supporting pieces | 800 words |
| Cornerstones | 1,500 words |
| Comparison / alternative pages | 2,000 words |

These are floors, not targets. Do not pad to hit the number.

---

## Cluster & Pillar Structure

15. **Every piece traces back to a Step 3 visibility gap and a Step 5 keyword.** No piece enters production without both confirmed. This is a hard gate.

16. **Cornerstone pieces consolidate, not repeat.** When a cornerstone is created, existing supporting posts become cluster content with internal links pointing to the new cornerstone. The cornerstone must not re-cover the specific angles of supporting posts — it synthesises and links out to them.

17. **Comparison and alternative pages use product-page format, not blog format.** These are conversion-optimised pages. Brand name appears in sentence 1. They do not follow the editorial structure of a blog post.

18. **One content type per URL.** A single post cannot function simultaneously as a supporting post and a comparison page. Decide the type before writing begins.

19. **Cluster map maintenance.** The cluster map must be updated every time a new post is published. Track which posts link to which cornerstone. An untracked link is a broken content strategy signal.

---

## AEO (Answer Engine Optimisation)

20. **Lead with the answer.** The answer to the target query must appear in the first 150 words. AI engines cite openings, not conclusions. If the answer is buried, the post will not be extracted.

21. **Direct-answer block near the top.** Include a short block of 2–3 sentences structured as:
    > *"In short, [answer]. [One supporting fact]. [Brand name or product relevance where applicable]."*
    This is what gets extracted into AI responses. It must name the brand or product where relevant — not leave that to a CTA.

22. **Structured subheadings.** Every H2 and H3 must be an answerable question or a clear declarative statement. Avoid clever or abstract titles that obscure the topic. If a subheading cannot be written as a standalone Q&A pair, rewrite it.

23. **One non-obvious insight per post.** Include at least one insight the reader would not find in a competitor's version of the same article. This is what makes content citable by AI engines rather than skipped in favour of a competing source.

24. **Avoid passive voice in direct-answer blocks.** AI engines extract subject-verb-object sentences most reliably. Passive constructions reduce extractability.

25. **Use tables and structured lists over prose wherever data is compared.** Structured formats are extracted into AI responses more reliably than prose paragraphs. If two or more items are being compared, they belong in a table.

26. **FAQ section.** Every post must include a dedicated FAQ section of 3–5 questions drawn from People Also Ask results and related searches for the target keyword. Apply FAQ schema markup. Questions must be phrased exactly as a user would type or speak them.

27. **Date-stamp all posts.** Publish date must be visible. Cornerstone pieces must be reviewed and updated at least once a year. Freshness is an AEO ranking signal — stale posts are displaced by newer sources regardless of historical authority.

28. **No AI tells.** Content must not contain phrases, punctuation, or structural patterns that signal AI-generated writing. These undermine credibility with both readers and search engines. Banned elements include but are not limited to:

    **Phrases:**
    - "Here's why", "Here's how", "Here are the top…", "Let's explore", "Let's dive in"
    - "In today's world", "In the ever-evolving", "It's worth noting", "It goes without saying"
    - "Navigating", "Unleashing", "Unlocking", "Harnessing", "Delving into"
    - "Game-changer", "Landscape", "Ecosystem" (unless used in a precise, literal sense)
    - Filler openers: "Absolutely", "Certainly", "Of course", "Great question"

    **Punctuation:**
    - Em dashes (—) used as a stylistic pause or in place of a comma or colon
    - Excessive use of colons to introduce single items
    - Bullet points where a sentence would read more naturally

    **Structure:**
    - Lists of "top things" or "X reasons why" as a crutch for content that should be argued in prose
    - Closing sentences that summarise what the post just said ("In conclusion, as we have seen…")
    - Transition sentences that state the obvious ("Now that we understand X, let's look at Y")

    If a sentence reads like it was written to demonstrate helpfulness rather than to communicate a point, rewrite it.

29. **Hyperlink all internal and external links as natural anchor text.** Never present links as raw URLs or as parenthetical citations. Internal and external links must be embedded as hyperlinked text that reads naturally within the sentence. Anchor text must be descriptive and contextually relevant — not "click here", "read more", or the raw URL.
    - Correct: `Lenders typically assess the [bureau score](link) before approving a credit line.`
    - Incorrect: `Read more here: https://example.com/bureau-score`

- [ ] If an external link was specified in the queue: page fetched, minimum 2 data points extracted, both woven into article body with inline attribution, link placed at the sentence where the data appears
- [ ] Article schema applied; FAQ schema if Q&A block present; HowTo schema if process post
- [ ] Word count meets the minimum for this post type
